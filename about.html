Our Story

Rooted in Kezi City

Kezi Pure began when three farming families banded together to protect ancestral moringa groves from commercial overharvesting. Today we cultivate 120 organic hectares overlooking the Tokwe River, where volcanic soils enrich every tree.

We plant cover crops, irrigate through rain-harvesting ponds, and use solar dryers to lower our carbon footprint. Each batch is traceable down to the plot and growers who tended it.

Regenerative Cultivation

Farmers prune by hand, mulch with fallen leaves, and compost using goat manure from nearby cooperatives. This regenerative loop ensures soil microbes thrive while water usage stays low.

  • Zero synthetic fertilizers or pesticides
  • Solar-powered processing houses
  • Fair-trade contracts for every grower

Craftsmanship & Community

Processing happens in our Kezi City atelier where women-led teams stone-mill leaves, cold-press seeds, and encapsulate powder under GMP certification. A portion of every sale funds seedling nurseries and nutrition programs for local schools.
